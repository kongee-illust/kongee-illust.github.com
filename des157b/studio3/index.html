Ultimately, I was inspired by my love for DIY (do-it-yourself) projects. From sloppy gingerbread houses to colorful finger paintings and submarine figures made of recycled water bottles, I was exposed to the idea of "doing" things myself. This mindset of "if I want it, then I'll make it" carried on as I developed my identity through my adolescence.

and it continues...

This mindset of "if I want it, then I'll make it" carried on as I developed my identity through my adolescence. Cooking, baking, crocheting, programming, and painting took over my free times, and I eventually filled up sketchbook pages faster than I reached my daily Move goal on the Apple Watch.

I fell in love with product design.

And when I found out that the Facebook Messenger interfaces, loading animations on websites, and the Visual Studio Code interface that I’ve been staring at for hours today were the product of hundreds of designers and their research and design, I decided that I can make it, too!

where I am today:

And now, through relevant courses like Interactive Media, Interactive Objects, UX/UI Design Methods, Human-Centered Design, and internships at various companies big and small, I design, plan, lead, and ultimately own projects guided by my passion for "doing" things myself.
