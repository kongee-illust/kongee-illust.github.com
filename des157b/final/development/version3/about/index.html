Why food?

Food as a love language

After moving to California in fifth grade, I felt that my identity as a Korean has been stripped away. From learning English just to get through the school day, learning the slangs, getting accustomed to food in America that just seemed so much more salty than greasy, I constantly worried that I was becoming a Korean American as my years spent in America bypassed the years spent in Korea. Thinking of my mom's Cheese Albap, I reminded myself of my roots through my comfort food.

And with my desire to create products to make a positive change in my community, I heard the sotires of my friends and family facing the same confusion in identity, and decided to create a commemorative website where we can celebrate the diversity of the Asian community at UC Davis, and reminisce the food that defined our childhood, comfort, and family.

It's through these foods that our parents and family have expressed their love. And now that you've heard the reason I created this project, perhaps you can...
